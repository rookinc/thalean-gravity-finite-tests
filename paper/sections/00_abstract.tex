\begin{abstract}
We study a repaired finite carrier graph \(X_\sigma\) with 900 vertices, obtained from a 15-slot carrier system with 60-state local fibers and a signed half-turn transport law. The paper makes no physical gravity claim. Its purpose is to isolate a finite graph-theoretic mechanism by which signed holonomy produces metric compression relative to an untwisted baseline \(X_0\).

For half-flip return pairs of the form \((s,x)\to(s,x+30)\), the repaired carrier graph shortens 294 of 900 return pairs and expands none. The strongest compression localizes on slots incident to odd carrier triangles. Ablating all odd carrier triangles collapses the strong compression profile. A full census over all \(\binom{30}{5}=142506\) five-edge carrier-sign toggles shows that odd carrier triangle count organizes a monotone median compression gradient, saturating at odd triangle counts 8 and 10.

The result is a bounded finite computational theorem: in this carrier system, odd carrier triangle holonomy acts as a local organizer of return-path metric compression.
\end{abstract}
