\section{Introduction}

This paper studies a finite mechanism of metric compression inside a signed carrier graph. The guiding question is simple:

\begin{quote}
Can a finite signed transport law create shorter return geometry?
\end{quote}

For the repaired \(K900\) carrier graph studied here, the answer is yes, and the mechanism is as follows. Closed signed transport can return to the same slot with a half-flipped local coordinate, and these return paths can be shorter than their untwisted counterparts. This is a finite graph-theoretic holonomy effect, not a force, field, or physical particle.

We define an \emph{odd carrier triangle} as the minimal closed witness of half-flip holonomy: a length-three carrier cycle whose signs sum to one modulo two. The full census below shows that odd carrier triangle count organizes a monotone metric compression gradient for half-flip return pairs.

The paper is deliberately bounded. It does not claim a physical theory of gravity. It does not identify a physical graviton. It records a finite theorem-grade mechanism in a specific repaired carrier graph.

The local 60-state carrier object is interpreted against the earlier dodecahedral transport construction \cite{cave2026dodecahedraltransport}. That prior work supplies conceptual ancestry for the thalion as a dodecahedral transport cell. The finite compression theorem proved here, however, depends only on the repaired \(K900\) payload and the Project 19 census and verifier artifacts.
