\section{Introduction}

This paper studies a finite mechanism of metric compression inside a signed carrier graph. The guiding question is simple:

\begin{quote}
Can a finite signed transport law create shorter return geometry?
\end{quote}

The answer, for the repaired \(K900\) carrier graph studied here, is yes. The relevant mechanism is not a force, field, or physical particle. It is a finite graph-theoretic holonomy effect: closed signed transport can return to the same slot with a half-flipped local coordinate, and these return paths can be shorter than their untwisted counterparts.

We call the central local witness an \emph{odd carrier triangle}: a length-three closed carrier cycle whose signs sum to one modulo two. The full census below shows that odd carrier triangle count organizes a monotone metric compression gradient for half-flip return pairs.

The paper is deliberately bounded. It does not claim a physical theory of gravity. It does not identify a physical graviton. It records a finite theorem-grade mechanism in a specific repaired carrier graph.
